## BODY

Seven patterns break regenerative projects.

Not bad intentions. Not wrong people. Not bad luck.

Structural gaps — specific, nameable, and almost always present before the crisis arrives.

I've been studying collapse in intentional communities, regenerative organizations, and New Earth projects for years. The same architecture fails in the same sequence across wildly different contexts.

This map names all seven.

---

**F2 usually starts it.**

Vision drift. People working hard, pulling in different directions. Every decision becomes a proxy conflict for something that was never actually agreed on.

**F3 follows.**

Governance shadows. Power lives somewhere but isn't accountable anywhere. The "flat" structure just means unspoken hierarchy with no process to surface it.

**Then F1 emerges.**

Interpersonal conflict. Which everyone calls a "people problem." It isn't. It's what happens when unresolved structure meets stressed humans.

**F5 starts quietly.**

The most committed people get tired. They stop raising things. They start performing engagement instead of feeling it. By the time they leave, the project has already lost its core.

**F6 accelerates it.**

Wrong-fit presence. Open admission without alignment criteria means the culture dilutes before it's strong enough to hold.

**F7 catches the survivors.**

The project grows. Or contracts. The organizational architecture doesn't move with it. Collapse zones sit at 8, 25, and 60 people — and most projects cross them without knowing.

**F4 is the floor.**

Financial fragility underlies everything. Scarcity triggers distrust. Distrust accelerates every other pattern.
